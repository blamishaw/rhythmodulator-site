The Rhythmodulator

An experimental instrument by Brendan Lamishaw

  • Learn more

Inspiration

As a long-time fan of hip-hop, electronic, and house music, I've always felt that drums truly dictated the genre of a track. The rest just seems to fall into place. The Rhythmodulator is designed to give users explicit control over a drum kit's kick and clap while movement implicitly controls a generative synth melody and bass stabs.

Architecture

The Rhythmodulator is composed of 4 sensors: two push buttons, a rotary encoder, and an ultrasonic sensor.

  • The buttons control the drum kit kick and clap respectively.
  • The rotary encoder controls the tempo of the generative synth chords as well as the speed of the drum kit hi-hats.
  • The ultrasonic sensor controls the pitch of the synth chords as well as plays the bass stab when close enough to an object.
Signal processing is done via an Arduino Uno and passed to Max/MSP to generate sound.

Sound Design & Playing Technique

The bass and chord synth sounds are generated wholly in Max/MSP through wave manipulation. These synths underwent much processing and many iterations to achieve their current sounds. The drum samples are taken from a stock Roland TR-909 drum kit.

The instrument is intended to held similar to a video-game controller. An individual will press each button with a different thumb and move the instrument up and down to trigger the ultrasonic sensor. The rotary encoder can be turned to increase or decrease the tempo of the synth chords and drum hats.
